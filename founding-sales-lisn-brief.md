# Founding Sales × LISN Agency — Solo Founder Sales SOP

**Your reality:** You run LISN Agency. Street interview UGC for Indian D2C brands. Your portfolio was built on barter and self-funding — not paid client work. You don't do free work. No free pilots, no performance guarantees.

**Indian D2C client reality:** They won't jump on a call first. They want to see pricing and details upfront. Most of the deal happens async over email/WhatsApp. The call might not happen until after they've already decided to buy.

**This SOP is adapted from Founding Sales (Kazanjy) for exactly this situation.**

---

## Chapter 1: Mindset

- **Activity above all.** 10 outreaches/day minimum.
- **Be direct.** "We shoot street interview UGC. Here's what we've made. Here's what it costs."
- **Build many shallow relationships.** 20+ conversations at once.
- **Record everything.** Spreadsheet or you're guessing.

---

## Chapter 2: Your Narrative

> "Indian D2C brands are running polished ads that blend into the feed. Street interview UGC is the antidote — proven globally by brands like Dollar Shave Club, Dr. Squatch, IL MAKIAGE. We've built a portfolio executing this format in India. Here's what we've made. Here's what we'd make for you."

**Three layers:**
1. **Problem** — Banner blindness. Rising ad costs. Creative fatigue.
2. **Format** — Street interviews. Real people, real reactions, no script.
3. **Your execution** — Portfolio proves you can shoot it. You funded it yourself because you believe in it.

**What you sell:** A production service with a flat fee. No guarantees, no free trials. "This is what we do, this is what it costs."

---

## Chapter 3: Sales Materials

1. **Instagram gallery** — Your only proof. Organise by vertical in your head.
2. **1-page summary** — Problem → Format → Portfolio → Price. Send this when they ask "what do you do?"
3. **Loom videos** — 2-min personalised Loom per prospect. Pull up their ads, show your work. Send as follow-up.
4. **Price sheet** — Be ready to share pricing the moment they ask. Don't hide it.

No slide decks. Speed is your advantage.

---

## Chapter 4: Prospecting

**Find brands that:** Are Indian D2C + run Meta ads + have a decision-maker you can reach.

**Where:**
- Instagram Sponsored posts
- Facebook Ad Library (facebook.com/ads/library)
- LinkedIn — search brand + "marketing" / "CMO"

**Prioritise:** Founder-led D2C brands. They decide faster.

**Spreadsheet columns:** Brand | Category | Contact | Title | LinkedIn | Active Ads? | Outreach Date | Response | Next Action

**Time:** 2 hrs/day morning before production.

## Chapter 5: Outreach — The 4-Touch Sequence

**Touch 1 — LinkedIn (Day 1)**
"Hey [Name], I run a UGC agency in Kochi. We shoot street interview creatives for Indian D2C brands. Would love to connect."

**Touch 2 — Email (Day 3)**
Subject: "What street interviews look like for D2C brands"
Body: "Hey [Name], I've been following [Brand]'s work. We specialise in street interview UGC — real reactions shot on the streets of Kochi/Bangalore. Here's a 30-second example: [link to best reel]. Want to see more? Happy to share pricing and details."

**Touch 3 — Follow-up (Day 7)**
"Hey [Name], circling back. Our campaigns start at ₹X for 5 creatives, 10-day turnaround. Here's a sample of what that looks like: [link]. If this is relevant, let me know and I'll send over a concept for [Brand]."

**Touch 4 — Breakup (Day 14)**
"Last one from me. If street interview content is ever relevant for [Brand], I'm here. If not, no hard feelings."

## Chapter 6: When They Respond — The Indian D2C Flow

**This is the key difference from Founding Sales.** Indian D2C clients will not "hop on a call." They want info + pricing first.

**When they reply with any variation of "send details" or "what's your pricing" or "tell me more":**

### Step 1 — Send exactly what they asked for + a sample

> "Hey [Name], thanks for the reply. Here's what we do:
>
> We produce street interview UGC — real reactions shot in Kochi/Bangalore. Each campaign includes 5 edited creatives ready for Meta/IG ads. Turnaround is 10 days from product receipt.
>
> Pricing starts at ₹X for a campaign.
>
> Here's the best example of our work: [link to reel]
>
> Let me know if you want to see more examples or want a concept for [Brand]."

**Do not** ask for a call here. Give them what they asked for. Leave the door open.

### Step 2 — They come back with more questions (they will)

Indian clients will ask: "Can you do X?" "What about Y?" "Can you share more samples?"

Answer each question directly. Then offer the next step:

> "Sure — here are 3 more examples across different formats: [links]. And here's what I'd propose for [Brand]: [1-2 sentence concept].
>
> If you're interested, I can send a formal proposal. Let me know."

### Step 3 — They ask for a proposal

Do NOT send a full proposal yet. Send a **lightweight concept note**:

> "Here's what I'd do for [Brand]:
>
> **Concept:** [Specific street interview concept for them]
> **Deliverables:** 5 x 30-60 second creatives
> **Investment:** ₹X
> **Timeline:** 10 days from product receipt
>
> If this looks good, I'll send the formal proposal and we can lock in a shoot date."

This keeps the conversation open. A formal proposal signals "take it or leave it." A concept note invites discussion.

### Step 4 — They negotiate on price (this is normal in India)

Don't drop price without getting something. Have anchors ready:

- "Standard campaign: 5 creatives for ₹X"
- "Smaller batch: 3 creatives for ₹Y"
- "3-month retainer: brings per-campaign cost to ₹Z"

> "I can do a smaller batch of 3 creatives for ₹Y if budget is tight right now. Or if you commit to 3 months, I can bring the per-campaign rate down."

### Step 5 — They say yes

Send a 1-page formal proposal + invoice. Payment terms: advance or 50% upfront.

Then get the kickoff on the calendar.

## The Full Pipeline From Response to Close (Indian Edition)

| Stage | What happens | What you send |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| 1. Lead replies | They ask "send details/pricing" | Pricing + portfolio link + brief description |
| 2. Interested | They ask follow-up questions | Answer directly + offer a concept |
| 3. Concept shared | They see what you'd do for them | 1-2 sentence concept note, no formal proposal |
| 4. Negotiation | They push on price | Options: smaller batch, retainer, or hold the price |
| 5. Verbal yes | They agree to move forward | Formal proposal + invoice |
| 6. Paid | Money in bank | Kickoff call scheduling |
| 7. Delivered | Content sent | Check-in after they run it |

**A call might never happen until Step 6 (kickoff). That's fine.**

---

## When a Call Does Happen (Optional Chapter 7)

If they want a call earlier, use this structure (from Founding Sales):

**0-3 min — Listen:** "How are your current creatives performing?"

**3-10 min — Show:** Play your best reel. Let the content sell.

**10-18 min — Make it specific:** "For [Brand], here's what I'd do..."

**18-25 min — Process + pricing:** State the price and shut up. Let them respond first.

**25-30 min — Ask:** "Is this something you'd like to move forward with?"

| They say | You say |
|----------|---------|

| "Too expensive" | "The standard is ₹X. I can do 3 creatives for ₹Y if that works better." |

| "Let me discuss with my team" | "Sure. Happy to join that discussion if it helps." |

## Chapter 8: Closing & Negotiation

**Closing sequence after verbal yes:**
1. **Within 2 hours** — Send formal proposal (1 page) + invoice
2. **Day 1** — "Here's the concept brief I'd use for the shoot"
3. **Day 3** — "I'm booking shoots for next week. Want me to lock in a slot?"
4. **Day 7** — Final follow-up
5. **Day 14** — Close as lost, re-engage in 60 days

**Close-losting:**
- Ask "What was the deciding factor?"
- "Budget" → re-engage 3 months
- "Not a priority" → re-engage 6 months
- "Went with someone else" → learn why, stay in touch
- "Not now" is often "yes later" — circle back quarterly

**Pipeline funnel:**
50 prospects → 30 outreached → 10 conversations → 5 proposals → 2 paid clients

---

## Chapter 9: Customer Success

**Delivery workflow:**
1. Kickoff (align on brand voice, messaging)
2. Receive product
3. Shoot day (1 day, 20-30 interactions)
4. Edit & deliver (3-5 creatives, 1 week)
5. Check-in: "How'd it perform? Want another batch?"

**Retention:**
- Deliver on time. Reliability is your differentiator.
- Send BTS clips they can post themselves.
- Check in monthly.
- Ask for referrals when they're happiest — right after they love the deliverables.

---

## Chapter 10: Managing Yourself

**Daily:** Morning 1-2 hrs prospecting → Midday production → Evening 15 min log activity

**Weekly:** Monday pipeline review. Friday reflect on what worked.

**Monthly:** Review numbers. If flat, change your approach.

**No one holds you accountable but you.**

## Chapters 11-13: Skip

Hiring, training, scaling — come back when you have 5-10 paid clients.

**Until then:** Prospect every morning. Give them pricing when they ask. Answer questions directly. Send concepts, not formal proposals. Deliver great work. Ask for referrals. Repeat.
