# Wispr Flow × LISN Agency
## Street UGC Campaign — Bangalore, India

---

### THE PROBLEM

India is Wispr Flow's **#2 market by usage** — but contributes only **~2% of in-app purchase revenue**.

The gap isn't product. It's *proof*.

Indian users (especially outside the US) need to *see* voice dictation working for people like them before they pay. Screenshots and feature lists don't convert the skeptical Indian consumer. Real, unfiltered, on-camera reactions do.

From Wispr's own data: growth in India accelerated **60%→100% MoM** after the Hinglish/Android launch. A street UGC campaign multiplies that trajectory by putting Flow in front of real Bangaloreans — their target audience — and capturing genuine reactions on camera.

---

### THE FORMAT: Street Interview UGC

We take Wispr Flow to **the streets of Bangalore**. Hand real people a phone loaded with Flow. Challenge them to dictate a message in their natural voice.

**What we capture:**

| Reaction Type | What Happens On Camera |
|--------------|----------------------|
| **The Skeptic** | "Voice typing never works for my accent" → Flow nails it |
| **The Hinglish User** | "Yaar kal meeting hai, actually 4 baje, slides bhej do" → clean text output |
| **The Developer** | Dictates a git commit message or code snippet in Whitefield |
| **The Student** | Dictates an assignment answer in mixed Hindi-English |
| **The Professional** | Dictates an email while walking on Church Street |

Each interaction is **unscripted, unrehearsed, real**.

**What you get back:**
- 20-30 raw street interactions filmed in 1 shoot day
- **5 edited creatives** (15-60s, vertical, Meta/IG ready)
- 10-business-day turnaround from product delivery
- Full commercial license for ad use

### THE STORYBOARD (5 Creatives)

**Creative 1 — "The Challenge" (Hook: curiosity)**
- Text overlay: "We asked Bangalore to speak faster than they type"
- Montage of 5 people being handed a phone, looking confused
- Cut to their reactions when the text appears

**Creative 2 — "The Accent Test" (Hook: relatability)**
- "Does voice dictation work with *your* accent?"
- 3 people with different Indian English/Hinglish accents try Flow
- Each one surprised when it works

**Creative 3 — "The Developer" (Hook: niche authority)**
- Whitefield/HSR Layout. Dev opens laptop.
- "git commit -m 'fixed the race condition in the payment webhook'"
- Flow tags the file in Cursor. Dev's face sells it.

**Creative 4 — "Hinglish Native" (Hook: vernacular)**
- Person speaks: "Kal client meeting hai, 3 baje, actually venue change hua hai, South Campus, Gate Number 2"
- Flow outputs clean formatted text
- "It understood *that*?!"

**Creative 5 — "The Verdict" (Hook: social proof)**
- Rapid cuts: 10 people, one word each
- "Fast." "Scary accurate." "Better than typing." "I'm installing this."
- End card: "Wispr Flow. Speak. Don't type. Available on Android, iOS, Mac, Windows."
