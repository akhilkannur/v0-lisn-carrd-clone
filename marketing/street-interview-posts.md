# Long-form post — street interview creatives (DTC founders & marketing teams)

Sources it's built on (so you can defend every claim):
- Meta's own analytics team on creative fatigue: avg user sees the same creative 4.2x, ~19% of impressions are 5+ repeat views, and there is NO "wear-in" — performance decays monotonically. (Analytics at Meta, Medium, 2023)
- Andromeda: Meta's AI delivery system rolled out late 2024, fully deployed Oct 2025; evaluates creative first, then finds the audience. (Search Engine Land / widely reported)
- Meta CPM up ~20% YoY in 2025, every single category. (Triple Whale, ~35,000 brands)
- Dr. Squatch: ~$4M → $100M, acquired by Unilever for ~$1.5B in 2025; built on raw, reaction-driven video.
- Dollar Shave Club "Order of the Blade": launch built around 23 real customers, not actors.

---

Most Indian D2C brands I talk to think their ads are underperforming because of targeting, or the algorithm, or the budget.

It's almost never that.

In late 2024, Meta quietly replaced its ad delivery system with one called Andromeda. By October 2025 it was running on every account. The old system took your targeting and went and found those people. Andromeda works backwards — it reads your creative first, then decides who to show it to. Your interest stacks and lookalikes are now closer to suggestions than instructions.

Translation: your creative *is* your targeting now. If the creative is weak, the machine has nothing to work with — no matter how clean your audience setup is.

And here's the part nobody wants to hear. Meta's own analytics team published their research on creative fatigue. Across the whole platform, the average person sees the same creative 4.2 times, and roughly one in five impressions is an ad someone has already seen five or more times. They also tested the old marketing belief that ads "warm up" before they wear out. They found zero evidence of it for performance ads. Every repeat exposure just makes your clicks and conversions more expensive. No honeymoon.

So the platform is burning through your creative faster than you can feel it, and it's hungry for fresh material constantly. Meanwhile CPMs on Meta went up around 20% last year — across every category. You're paying more to reach the same people who are getting bored of your ad faster.

That's the real problem. Not targeting. Creative supply.

Now, why street interviews specifically.

A polished studio ad has one job it structurally cannot do: not look like an ad. Your customer's brain has been trained by ten thousand identical product-shot-plus-voiceover-plus-20%-off ads to skip that exact layout on sight. Scripted UGC is half a step better, but people can hear the script now. The "I was skeptical at first, but…" beat gives it away in two seconds.

A street interview removes the one thing you can fake — the script — and captures the one thing you can't: a stranger's involuntary reaction. The pause. The surprised face. The honest "wait, this is actually good." That involuntary moment is the pattern interrupt. It's what makes a thumb stop. And a thumb stopping is the only signal Andromeda actually rewards.

This isn't a hunch. Dr. Squatch went from roughly $4M to $100M on the back of raw, reaction-led video and got bought by Unilever for about $1.5B last year. Dollar Shave Club built an entire razor launch around 23 real customers instead of actors. The format is proven with serious money — just not in India yet. Most brands here are still running the polished stuff those brands walked away from years ago.

It also fits how the platform now works, mechanically. One street shoot day gives you 15 real reactions — enough raw material to cut 8-10 genuinely different ads, not ten recolors of the same one. That matters, because Meta's system groups near-duplicate ads together and throttles them; a new background colour doesn't count as a new ad. Real variety is the only thing that feeds a system that eats creative for breakfast.

Let me be honest about what this won't do. It won't fix a product people don't want. It won't rescue a broken offer. It's one weapon, not the whole arsenal. What it does is buy you attention in a feed engineered to ignore you, and give the top of your funnel an actual shot.

If your ROAS is soft and every ad in your category looks the same, your media buyer isn't the problem. Your creative is invisible.

Fix that first. Everything downstream gets cheaper.
